Pistachio Cake

Home

Decsription

This Pistachio Cake boasts a soft, moist crumb with a pronounced pistachio flavor, achieved by using nearly double the amount of pistachios compared to typical recipes. Toasting the pistachios before grinding enhances their nutty essence, ensuring each bite is rich and flavorful. Topped with a light and tangy Cream Cheese Whip, this cake offers a perfect balance of earthiness and subtle sweetness, making it an elegant choice for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups (185g) plain flour (all-purpose flour)
  • 1¼ teaspoons baking powder
  • ¼ teaspoon baking soda (bicarb)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • 150g unsalted pistachios (shelled, unsalted)
  • 1 cup (220g) white sugar
  • 185g unsalted butter, softened
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon almond extract (optional, for enhanced flavor)
  • ⅔ cup (160ml) milk

Steps

  1. Prepare the pan: Preheat the oven to 180°C/350°F (160°C fan-forced). Grease and line a 20 cm / 8" round cake pan with baking paper.
  2. Toast the pistachios: Spread 125g of unsalted pistachio kernels on a baking tray and toast in the oven for 12 minutes until fragrant and crisp. Allow them to cool completely.
  3. Make pistachio powder: Once cooled, blitz the toasted pistachios into a powder using a food processor or blender. Be careful not to over-process into a paste; some small lumps are acceptable.
  4. Combine dry ingredients: In a large bowl, whisk together the pistachio powder, 1 cup plain flour, 1¼ teaspoons baking powder, ¼ teaspoon baking soda, and ¼ teaspoon salt. Use your fingers to break up any clumps of pistachio powder.
  5. Combine wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together ¾ cup plain yogurt (at room temperature), 60g melted unsalted butter (slightly cooled), ¼ cup plain oil, ¾ cup caster sugar, ½ teaspoon vanilla extract, and 1 large egg (at room temperature) until smooth.
  6. Combine wet and dry mixtures: Pour the wet ingredients into the bowl with the dry ingredients. Use a rubber spatula to mix until the flour is almost incorporated.
  7. Add food coloring (optional): Add 4 drops of green food coloring to the batter and continue mixing until the batter is mostly lump-free. Small lumps will dissolve during baking.
  8. Transfer to pan and bake: Scrape the batter into the prepared cake pan and spread it out evenly. Bake for 40 minutes, rotating the pan at the 30-minute mark to ensure even browning. The cake is done when a skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.
  9. Cool the cake: Allow the cake to cool in the pan for 10 minutes, then turn it out onto a cooling rack and let it cool completely (about 1 hour).
  10. Prepare the Cream Cheese Whip: In a bowl, beat 100g of room-temperature cream cheese on medium-high speed for about 2 minutes until light and creamy. Add ¾ cup thickened cream, 3 tablespoons caster sugar, ½ teaspoon vanilla extract, 2 teaspoons lemon juice (optional), and a pinch of salt. Beat until the mixture is softly whipped and spreadable, about 1 minute.
  11. Frost the cake: Once the cake is completely cooled, spread the Cream Cheese Whip over the top, creating swirls with a spatula.
  12. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle the frosted cake with finely chopped pistachios. Slice and serve.
