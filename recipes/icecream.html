Description

This Homemade Vanilla Ice Cream is a rich, creamy, and indulgent treat that's surprisingly easy to make. With just five simple ingredients—heavy cream, whole milk, cane sugar, vanilla extract, and a pinch of salt—you can create a dessert that's far superior to store-bought versions. The recipe requires minimal hands-on time, making it perfect for summer days when you crave a cool, homemade delight. Whether enjoyed on its own or adorned with your favorite toppings, this vanilla ice cream is sure to become a household favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups heavy cream
  • 1½ cups whole milk
  • ⅔ cup cane sugar
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • ⅛ teaspoon sea salt

Steps

  1. At least 12 hours before making the ice cream, freeze the bowl of your ice cream maker.
  2. In a medium saucepan over medium-low heat, combine the heavy cream, whole milk, cane sugar, vanilla extract, and sea salt. Warm for 5 minutes, whisking often, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture is warmed through.
  3. Transfer the mixture to a heatproof bowl, cover, and chill in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours or overnight.
  4. Once chilled, remove the mixture from the refrigerator and whisk well.
  5. Churn the mixture in your ice cream maker according to the manufacturer's instructions, typically for 20 to 30 minutes.
  6. The ice cream will have a soft texture after churning. For a firmer consistency, transfer it to an airtight container and freeze for 2 to 4 hours before serving.
  7. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the freezer for up to a month. If the ice cream is too firm to scoop straight from the freezer, let it sit at room temperature for a few minutes to soften.
